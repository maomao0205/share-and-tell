Why It's Complicated

Three Tensions This Case Exposes

Tension 01

Safety vs. Access

  • Anthropic: jailbreak only surfaces simple, known vulnerabilities — other public models can do the same
  • If this standard applies industry-wide, it effectively halts all frontier model releases
  • Who sets the guardrail bar?
Tension 02

Amazon's Triple Role

  • Largest investor in Anthropic
  • Primary cloud provider (AWS)
  • The one who reported to the government
  • Responsible disclosure — or competitive interest?
Tension 03

First-Ever Commercial AI Export Control

  • First time in U.S. history export controls used on a commercial AI model
  • Applied only to Anthropic — not OpenAI, Google, or Meta
  • A precedent with almost no legal framework around it

Course Themes: Week 4 AI Safety · Week 6 Regulatory & Policy Frameworks

The First Government Shutdown of a Commercial AI Model Won't Be the Last

01 · Guardrails Stronger models, weaker guardrails

Fable's guardrails weren't beaten by a sophisticated attack — bypassed by "fix this code." Guardrails rely on the model judging user intent. That's fundamentally unreliable.

02 · Governance Export controls, not legislation

90-minute notice vs. years of legislative process. Faster, less transparent, almost no appeals. A new model of AI governance is taking shape — outside the courts.

03 · Access AI access as geopolitical resource

You're not a U.S. citizen — your AI tools can be cut off because of your nationality. AI access is becoming a geopolitical lever, not just a product.

"This isn't just a story about one model being taken offline.
It's a preview of how governments will govern AI —
through national security, not legislation."
